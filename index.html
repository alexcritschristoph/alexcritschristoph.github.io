Alex Crits-Christoph

I am an undergraduate in Biophysics at the Johns Hopkins University. My interests include biotechnology, ecology and evolution, web development, bioinformatics, and statistics. I'm also a co-founder of SequeBase, a bioinformatics platform for data search and analysis.

Experience

I was accepted to Google Summer of Code for summer 2014, and I am currently building an exploratory web interface for the Cancer Variants Database (Can-VD), a dataset created by the Bader Lab at the University of Toronto.

For the last two years I have been working with Dr. Jocelyne DiRuggiero's lab at Johns Hopkins University in microbial genomics on computational genomics and ecology of extremophile communities and bacterial + archaeal microbiomes.

In 2013, I worked with Dr. Rachel Karchin's lab at the Institute for Computational Medicine on a project analyzing networks of mutation relationships in TEM B-lactamase from an ancestral phylogeny in order to describe the evolution of antibiotic resistance.

In Summer 2011, I collaborated with the Plotkin Research Group at the University of Pennsylvania on computing entropy of the Influenza HA protein using protein sequence and structure data.
